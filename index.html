/FUTURE/...

A long-term personal goal of mine is to bridge my background in music with technology. I'm especially interested in working on AI based VST plug-ins that generate curated and unique performances and acoustics, analyzing the iterations that the users interact most with and save to their environment, and releasing improvements based on those findings.

I’m curious about the implications of AI and big data on art, and not only to generally understand creativity as a standalone mechanism better but its importance and relationship with social behaviors as well.

Ultimately, my hope is to study attributes that may contribute to "successful" musical compositions and develop innovative products that engage users from all backgrounds to help reduce barriers to developing creativity skills.

/STORY/WHY_THE_CHANGE

I was born and raised in New York City to artistic Macedonian immigrants and always knew I had a talent for observation, interpretation, and expression. Until recently, the medium that attracted me most for exploring these instincts was music, and I went on to complete a BA with Berklee College of Music. Although music is rich in theory that is rooted in math and physics, my need to learn about and interact with the world evolved to require a more quantitative relationship as I grew through my various projects, gigs, and existential searches. After an entire life of thinking I was “not a math person”, I’m grateful to see I was only limiting myself with that mentality and to have had one amazing passion lead me to another.
